The Unity Day

Parade, Uncle Max commemoration

At the end of the afternoon, around 6pm, Sydney hour, will start the parade.The whole cartage will go down the Macquarie St all the way from the Archibald Memorial fountain up to the Opera House, and will be composed of aborigines dancing and militarians in line. We believe in this show as an opportunity to strengthen the national sense of belonging of every citizen. After this national show-off, we will follow up with a mourning time of remembrance to commemorate the saddest time of our history. Through this one minute of silence, we will remember every aborigine persecuted, badtreated or disadvantaged, the kids of the stolen generation and their family members, but also Uncle Max , who was known as one of the wisest and most advisefull person in Australia. This aborigine died around a year ago and has spent his life trying to make other people's better through reconnection with nature. This mourning time will end with the national band interpreting the national anthem along with aborigines playing the didgeridoo. The final purpose behind this whole display is to remember and accept the ashaming parts of our history to unite and move forward.
